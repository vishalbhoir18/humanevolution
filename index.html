04

The story in the strata

About 15 mya, primates called Dryopithecus and Ramapithecus were living. They were hairy and moved like gorillas and chimpanzees. Ramapithecus was the more man-like of the two, while Dryopithecus stayed closer to the apes.

Fossils of man-like bones found in Ethiopia and Tanzania carried hominid features, suggesting that around 3–4 mya man-like primates were walking in eastern Africa. They were probably no taller than four feet, yet they walked upright.

By 2 mya, the australopithecines were living in the East African grasslands. The evidence shows they hunted with stone weapons but ate mostly fruit. Among the discovered bones, some were clearly different — and this creature, the first true hominid, was named Homo habilis, with a brain capacity of 650–800 cc. It probably did not eat meat.

Fossils found in Java in 1891 revealed the next stage, Homo erectus, around 1.5 mya, carrying a larger brain of about 900 cc. Homo erectus probably ate meat.

The Neanderthal man, with a brain of 1400 cc, lived across the Near East and central Asia between 100,000 and 40,000 years ago. They used hides to cover their bodies and buried their dead.

Homo sapiens arose in Africa, spread across the continents, and developed into distinct races. During the ice age, between 75,000 and 10,000 years ago, modern Homo sapiens emerged. Cave art appeared about 18,000 years ago; agriculture followed around 10,000 years ago, and human settlements began. The rest is history.

Each layer carries a little more brain than the one beneath it — from roughly 500 cc to 1450 cc across the climb.
